# Ethics and Misuse Boundaries

Tracewright is a tool for careful reading, not exposure.

## Core Rule

Do not use this tool to accuse, shame, expose, or rank people based on suspected AI use.

Evidence Cards are not suspicion cards. They are meant to preserve observations, source excerpts, alternative explanations, and next checks. They must not be turned into a list of people to accuse, discipline, shame, or rank.

## Intended Uses

- slowing down premature judgment
- documenting uncertainty
- separating evidence from intuition
- preserving alternative explanations
- supporting provenance-aware editorial review
- training reviewers to inspect AI-mediated text carefully

## Non-Uses

- identifying whether a person used AI
- policing private correspondence
- generating accusations
- producing authenticity scores
- replacing consent or human review
- making employment, legal, academic, reputational, provenance, publication, or financial decisions without qualified human review

## Privacy

Public demos should use synthetic, anonymized, or consent-safe materials only. Private letters, names, identifiable details, and raw correspondence should not be published without explicit consent.

## Human Judgment

A reviewer should always distinguish:

- what the text says
- what is known about how the text was made
- what is inferred from style or context
- what remains unknown

The purpose is not to decide faster. The purpose is to decide more carefully.

## High-Impact Decisions

If a Tracewright review may affect a person's job, legal status, academic standing, reputation, publication, financial interests, ownership claim, or professional evaluation, the map should be treated only as preparation for qualified human review. It is not a decision system.

## Misuse Examples

Do not use Tracewright to:

- label someone as deceptive because a text looks polished
- treat rough writing as proof that a person wrote without assistance
- publish private or identifiable materials without consent
- turn Evidence Cards into a suspicion list
- collapse style signals, factual reliability, and moral judgment into one score
